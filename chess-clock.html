Free Online Chess Clock — Fischer, Bronstein and US Delay

A free chess clock that runs directly in your browser. No download or app installation needed. Supports Fischer increment, Bronstein delay and US delay time controls. Perfect for tournament play, coaching sessions and home practice. Works on all mobile phones and tablets.

Chess Clock with Fischer Increment

Fischer increment adds time to your clock after every move. The most widely used time control in tournaments worldwide including FIDE and USCF events.

Bronstein Delay Chess Clock

Bronstein delay gives back only the time you actually used — your clock never grows beyond its starting value. Ideal for correspondence and long format games.

US Delay Chess Clock

US delay freezes the clock for a set number of seconds before it starts counting down. The standard format for USCF rated games and American tournaments.

How does extra time work each move?
Most Common
Fischer Increment
A fixed number of seconds is added to your clock immediately after you press it — every single move, regardless of how fast or slow you played.
e.g. 10+5 → you get +5s every move. Move in 3s? Your clock grows.
USCF Standard
US Delay
Your clock waits for a set number of seconds before it starts counting down. You always get the full delay — but unused delay time is never added to your clock.
e.g. 10 d/5 → clock freezes for 5s then counts down. Total never increases.
Balanced
Bronstein Delay
After you press your clock, you get back only the time you actually used — up to the delay maximum. Move fast and you recover it all. Move slow and you only get part of it back.
e.g. 10|5 → used 3s? Get 3s back. Used 8s? Get only 5s back. Clock never grows.
